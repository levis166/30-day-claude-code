## Billiani: Craft, Character & Commercial Versatility

Billiani continues to be one of the most compelling stories in European furniture design. Founded in 1911 and rooted in the Manzano craft tradition of northeastern Italy, their 2026 collection reinforces why this brand commands attention in hospitality and commercial specification.

What stood out to our team this year was Billiani's continued investment in the intersection of **artisanal quality and scalability** — a critical consideration when specifying across a hotel dining room, a corporate lounge, or a multi-site restaurant group. Their new seating introductions play with curvature and tactile upholstery in ways that feel warm and residential, yet are engineered for the rigours of commercial use.

The palette this season leans into earthy, grounded tones — rich terracottas, warm taupes, and deep forest greens — reflecting a broader industry move toward interiors that feel anchored and enduring rather than trend-chasing. For hospitality designers in particular, this is a welcome direction.

---

## Infiniti: Bold Forms for Forward-Thinking Spaces

If Billiani speaks to warmth and tradition, **Infiniti** speaks to ambition. Their 2026 showcase in Milan demonstrated why they remain a go-to for specifiers working on contemporary office environments, public spaces, and high-design hospitality concepts.

Infiniti's strength lies in their ability to push form without sacrificing function. This year, the brand leaned into **fluid, architecturally expressive silhouettes** — pieces that feel like deliberate design statements within a space rather than background elements. Stackability, modularity, and sustainability credentials were woven throughout, answering the very real demands placed on contract furniture in 2026.

The team were particularly drawn to their new lounge and breakout seating — categories that have never been more important as workplace design continues to evolve away from the traditional desk-centric model.

---

## What the Trends Mean for Your Next Project

Across both brands — and across Milan more broadly — a few consistent themes emerged that are directly relevant to commercial specification right now:

- **Biophilic warmth** continues to dominate, with natural materials and organic forms leading across categories
- **Longevity over novelty** — clients and designers alike are prioritising pieces built to last, both physically and aesthetically
- **Flexibility** remains non-negotiable in workplace and hospitality environments that need to work harder across more use cases
